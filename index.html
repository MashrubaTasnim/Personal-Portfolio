About Me

Hello! I'm Mashruba Tasnem Oishi, a Computer Science & Engineering graduate of IUBAT (CGPA: 3.60/4.00). My undergraduate thesis focused on Explainable AI — building a leakage-free, SHAP-explainable network intrusion detection framework on the UNSW-NB15 dataset, benchmarking Random Forest and XGBoost classifiers.

I recently completed a Web Developer Internship at SoftDeft, where I designed and built a full-stack Animal Rescue & Sanctuary Management System with role-based portals, secure CRUD operations, and AI-assisted features.

Fueled by my passion for AI, Cybersecurity, and Software Development, I enjoy turning complex ideas into practical, user-centered solutions. I aim to use technology for the betterment of mankind, animals, and the environment, creating impactful systems that promote safety, kindness, and sustainability. I'm also actively pursuing opportunities to teach core Computer Science courses and mentor undergraduate researchers.

Outside the world of technology, I am an animal enthusiast, artist, and lifelong learner who loves painting, reading, and exploring creative ideas.
